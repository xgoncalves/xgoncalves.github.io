Editor's note

We can't put it together. It is together. The Whole Earth Catalog taught a generation that the right tools, clearly reviewed, could change how people live and work. This page applies the same principle to product design: not glamorous white space for its own sake, but dense, useful information you can act on.

I'm Xavier Gonçalves, a designer with a habit of finding the real problem behind the stated one. I work where business meets engineering meets human behaviour, and I believe the best interface is the one you forget was designed at all. The entries below are not services in the agency sense. They are access points: ways we might collaborate.

Information wants to be useful. If something here resonates, the mail-order form at the back of this catalog is always open.

Problem Framing

Core method

Before any wireframe: map the system. Who benefits, who pays, what breaks at scale. The stated problem is rarely the real one.

"A rare designer who asks 'should we build this?' before 'how should it look?'" · The kind of PM you want

Design Philosophy

Operating manual

Inevitable, not invisible. Good design removes friction so completely you forget someone designed it. Evidence over opinion. Systems over screens.

II

Craft & Making

pp. 12–27

Product Design

Full cycle

Strategy to high-fidelity prototype. User flows, interaction design, visual polish. I stay in the room through implementation, because that's where designs live or die.

"Survives contact with reality. Highly recommended for teams tired of pretty decks that never ship." · Catalog review

Interaction Design

Behaviour layer

States, transitions, edge cases. The unglamorous work of making software feel like it has common sense. Micro-interactions that reward attention without demanding it.

"Every loading state considered. Every empty state intentional. Refreshingly thorough." · Catalog review

Prototyping

Prototype · Code

Clickable prototypes for testing. Sometimes code prototypes for proving feasibility. The medium follows the question, not the other way around.

"Prototypes that engineers respect. A rarer commodity than you'd think." · Catalog review

Visual Design

Surface & depth

Typography, colour, spacing: the grammar of trust. Visual systems that communicate before a single word is read. Restraint as a feature.

"Clean without being sterile. Distinctive without being loud." · Catalog review
III

Systems & Standards

pp. 28–40

Design Systems

Scale tool

Components that scale. Tokens that make semantic sense. Documentation people actually read. A shared language between design and engineering, not a library nobody maintains.

"Consistency without creativity's death. The balance is hard; this catalog entry delivers." · Catalog review

Component Libraries

Atomic → organism

Built for real constraints: accessibility, i18n, theming, responsive behaviour. Components tested against production edge cases, not ideal-state mockups.

"Finally, a system where 'button' doesn't mean four different things in four different files." · Catalog review

Design Tokens

Single source

Semantic naming. Platform-agnostic export. Colour, spacing, typography, motion: defined once, consumed everywhere. The plumbing that makes redesigns possible.

"The unsexy infrastructure that saves quarters of rework. Essential." · Catalog review
IV

Communications

pp. 41–55

UX Research

Evidence kit

Interviews, usability testing, heuristic analysis. Replacing opinions with evidence, and building the case to convince stakeholders to do the same.

"Turns 'I think users want…' into 'we observed users doing…' Invaluable in roadmap debates." · Catalog review

User Interviews

Listening tool

Structured conversations that surface mental models, workarounds, and unspoken needs. Not focus groups: deep dives with the people who actually use the thing.

"Asks the questions nobody thought to ask. Finds the insight hiding in plain sight." · Catalog review

Stakeholder Alignment

Translation layer

Workshops, journey maps, shared artifacts. Making the invisible visible so teams move in the same direction, even when they disagree on the destination.

"The diplomatic skill of a translator, the rigour of an analyst. Keeps projects from derailing." · Catalog review
